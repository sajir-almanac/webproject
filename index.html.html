The Constellations in the night sky


There are 88 constellation in the modern night sky. For centuries, human beings have looked
at arrangements of stars in the sky and attributed the different patterns to resemble mythical figures.
The presence of constellations stands as testamony to human creativity as well as ingenuity when it comes to navigation.


Claudius Ptolemy: The ancient astronomer


The most significant name in astronomy in the ancient world was Claudius Ptolemy.
He compiled these arrangement of heavenly bodies in the sky to 48 constellations.
Today, the modern conestellation set includes all of his 48 designs, highlighting
his significance in both ancient astronomy and modern astronomy.


The three largest constellations are:
  • Hydra
  • Virgo
  • Ursa Major
The three smallest constellations are:
  1. Crux
  2. Equuleus The Little Horse
  3. Sagitta
